Brad Fraser's Cold Meat Party, set in Manchester and first staged there, has much liveliness but little organic life, possibly because its principal character is dead. He was a Canadian novelist (his coffin's onstage) who controlled his friends in life and is intent on doing so after death. There are funny lines and touching scenes, but the author can't make box-set comedy conventions work consistently. The best thing is the acting, with Ross Manson and Amy Price-Francis especially fine. (Factory Theatre, 125 Bathurst St., until Oct. 31).
