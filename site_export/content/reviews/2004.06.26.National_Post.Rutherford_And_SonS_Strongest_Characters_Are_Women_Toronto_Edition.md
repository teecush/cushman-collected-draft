In this play from 1912, there's trouble at a Yorkshire glass- making firm run on patriarchal principles -- though the strongest and cleverest characters are, like the author, Githa Sowerby, women. It's a gritty and compelling piece, with a strong narrative that ends with a woundingly ironic reversal. Michael Ball rises finely to the role of the tyrannical Rutherford; as his daughter, whose griefs and accusations come pouring out white-hot, Kelli Fox is stunning. (Court House Theatre, Niagara-on-the-Lake. Runs to Oct. 9. 1-800- 511-7429. www.shawfest.com.)
