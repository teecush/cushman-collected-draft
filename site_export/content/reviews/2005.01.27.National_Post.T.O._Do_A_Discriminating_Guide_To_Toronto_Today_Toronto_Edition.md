CRITICS PICKS

FILM

Cinematheque Ontario (317 Dundas St. W.) wraps Sergio Leone retrospective tonight at 6:30 p.m. with the director's final film, the epic (almost four-hour!) Once Upon a Time in America, starring Robert De Niro and James Woods. $7.25 for seniors/members, $11.50 for non-members. Tomorrow at 6:30 p.m., filmmaker Atom Egoyan moderates A Marriage of Minds: Directors and Cinematographers, featuring writer/actor/director Don McKellar, director Daniel Roby and cinematographer Eric Cayla discussing the bond between directors and directors of photography. $7, $6 for seniors. At 8:30 p.m., McKellar's Childstar kicks off Cinematheques top 10 Canadian films of 2004. (See review in tomorrows Post.) $7, $6 for seniors. Information, 416-968-FILM, www.bell.ca/cinematheque or www.topten.ca.

THEATRE

It's a great moment in Toronto for seamless ensemble theatre. You have a week-and-a-half to catch A Whistle in the Dark (Berkeley Street Upstairs), a 40-year-old play that already packs the weight of a classic, written by the Irish Thomas Murphy, directed by the Irish Jason Byrne, with a flawless Canadian cast; the constant threat of violence puts you on the edge of your seat, richness of character keeps you there. 'da Kink in My Hair (Princess of Wales), by Trey Anthony, has its imperfections, but its warm-hearted semi- musical entertainment is the nearest weve yet come to a theatrical equivalent of Caribana. Even the cliched parts feel drawn from life; the engaging author and the shattering d.bi young stand out in an excellent all-female cast. Trout Stanley (Factory) is quantitatively more modest, having only three performers, but they are, under Eda Holmes direction, impeccable. The quality of the text doesn't hurt either.

SPORTS

If you're still looking for hockey-viewing opportunities in greater Toronto now that the pros are on the verge of packing it in, Ontario University mens hockey is into the last three weeks of the regular season and both York University and the University of Toronto have weekend games against division opponents. Both teams lead their divisions (York, 8-5-2-1 atop the Midwest; Toronto, 9-6- 1-0 in the Mideast). On Friday, York plays Guelph at 7:30 p.m. at The Ice Gardens on campus. Yorks Lions boast one of the players featured on CBC's Making The Cut, goalie Scott Della Vedova, who splits time with Kevin Druce. Toronto, which plays Queen's on Saturday at 7:30 p.m. at Varsity Arena on campus, has the leagues top scorer, Ian Malcolm, who has 19 goals and 11 assists in 16 games. York information, 416-736-5183; Toronto information, 416-978- 5845.

CLASSICAL

DEBUSSY QUARTET

Music Toronto presents the Toronto debut of the Debussy Quartet. This foursome from Lyon, France, will present works of Mozart, Faure and Belgian composer Guillaume Lekeu. Tonight at 8 p.m. $39-$43, $5 for students and half price for an accompanying adult. At the Jane Mallett Theatre, 27 Front St. E. 416-366-7723. www.stlc.com.

DUO IO

Duo Io presents French Twist tonight at The Music Gallery. Duo Phoebe Tsang, violin, and Julien Le Blanc, piano, will perform the fusion of French and French-Canadian musical works. 8 p.m. 197 John St. $15 regular, $10 for members and $5 for students and seniors. 416-204-1080. www.musicgallery.org.

GALLERIES

THE AGO

Christo and Jeanne-Claude: Works from the Weston Collection is on now at the Art Gallery of Ontario. Offering a captivating look at the development of some of the largest and most spectacular environmental artworks of our time. Runs to May 15; The Present Tense series of contemporary art features the work of Kori Newkirk in his first solo Canadian museum exhibition. Runs to April 24. The AGO is open Tuesday to Sunday. 317 Dundas St. W. 416-979-6660 or www.ago.net.

THE OTHER LANDSCAPES

McMichael Gallery presents an exhibition by The Group of Seven and Tom Thomson designed to show the artists produced more than images of virgin wilderness. Runs to Feb. 13. Open daily 10 a.m. to 4 p.m., $12-$30. Islington Avenue, north of Major Mackenzie Drive, in Kleinburg, Ont. 905-893-1121.

NFB Film Thursdays presents Rare Birds and a special sneak preview of Beowulf and Grendel tonight. The former is the story of a chef who is struggling with a failed marriage and a failing restaurant. THEATRE

A FLEA IN HER EAR

Stage Centre Productions presentation of A Flea in Her Ear begins tonight and runs to Feb. 12. This comedic story begins to go awry when a middle-class insurance salesman becomes impotent, leading his wife to believe he has taken a mistress. At the Fairview Library Theatre, 35 Fairview Mall Dr. Today to Saturday at 8 p.m.. Sunday matinees at 2 p.m. $25, $20, seniors, $15, students. 416-299- 5557. www.stagecentreproductions.com.

THE BROTHERHOOD OF MANNS

Alliesallover Theatre presents The Brotherhood of Manns tonight through Feb. 5. This is a dark comedy about the bonds of brotherhood. At the Alchemy Theatre, 133 Tecumseth St. Wednesday through Sunday, 8 p.m, with matinees on Saturday, Sunday and Feb. 4 to 5 at 2 p.m. $10. 416-836-0289.

TAKE ME OUT

Richard Greenberg's Take Me Out is showing now at CanStage Theatre. The play tells the story of a rich, handsome baseball player who outs" himself at the height of his career. Runs until Feb. 12. Monday to Saturday 8 p.m., Wednesday matinees, 1:30 p.m. and Saturday matinees, 2 p.m. 27 Front St. E. $36 to $80. 416-368- 3110. www.canstage.com.

This year's season includes You Never Can Tell, The Constant Wife, Gypsy, Journeys End, Major Barbara, Something on the Side, The Autumn Garden and more, as well as the Reading Series and the Musical Reading Series. Call 905-468-2172 or visit www.shawfest.com.

Tickets are now on sale for the 2005 Stratford Festival. This years season begins on April 19 with Into the Woods by Stephen Sondheim and includes, among others, Shakespeare's The Tempest and As You Like It, Tennessee Williams' Cat on a Hot Tin Roof, Dostoevsky's The Brothers Karamazov and Wingfield's Inferno. The festival runs April 19 through Nov. 6. $20 to $114. 1-800-567-1600. www.stratfordfestival.ca.
