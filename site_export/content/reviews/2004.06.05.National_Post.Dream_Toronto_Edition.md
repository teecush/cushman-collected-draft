Festival Theatre, Stratford.

Leon Rubin's production, set in a rainforest with trailing tendrils (not to mention vertiginous trapezes), has no detectible overall vision: Its considerable pleasures have to be taken as they come. Among them are young lovers who hold attention from first to last; an Oberon (Jonathan Goad) in deft control of every mood and nuance; and a superb Bottom (Thom Marriott) who leads an exceptionally funny Pyramus-and-Thisbe show. But cutting references to Athens and the tradesmen severs the play from its roots.
