Juliet (and Romeo), a truncated eight-actor Romeo and Juliet, aims to tell the old story from Juliet's point of view -- a task already largely accomplished by Shakespeare himself. It works, more or less, despite uneven performances from the leads and inconsistent design. The production is good at mapping out relationships and the fights, mainly with knives, are tense and very nasty. Juliet's dad is now a single parent, which is interesting. (Lorraine Kimsa Theatre for Young People. Until Dec. 11. 416-862-2222.)
