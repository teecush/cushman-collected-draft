A pleasing, light-hearted blazer-and-boater production of one of Shakespeare's earliest comedies, not too rich in subtext or plot complications. The two ladies have better chances than the two gents, and take them. A rotating team of canines in little white collars (Shakespeare in the Ruff?) shares the role of Crab the dog, but steals no scenes from their master, Launce (Jameson Kraemer), who gets all of his laughs without pushing for them, and doubles on trombone. (Withrow Park, Danforth and Logan avenues. 647-896-9680. Runs until Sept. 6.)
