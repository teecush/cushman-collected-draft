In Private Jokes, Public Places, Moshe Safdie, son of a real- life famous architect, has written a play in which two fictitious famous architects pass judgment, nastily, on a student's final-year project. It suffers from a damnable lack of conflict: There is this idealistic woman who turns into Joan of Arc and there are these well- fed establishment bullies. It's obvious whom we're going to side with. Alisa Palmer's production enthusiastically pushes the play even further into caricature. (Tarragon Theatre, until Oct. 24. 416- 531-1827.)
