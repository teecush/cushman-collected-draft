A lunchtime production of J.M. Synges short piece, which, like most of his plays, depicts the peasantry of rural Ireland as a voluble set of cunning loons. Three vagrants battle a parish priest. Synge's great strength is his lack of sentimentality; his great weakness, though it's often reckoned a strength, is the elaboration of his style. Words in plays should be weapons, but in this production the delivery is in a style it's impossible to resist describing as Synge-song. (Court House Theatre, Niagara-on-the- Lake. 1-800-511-7429. Runs until Sept. 5).
