This is Kelli Fox's season for being incandescent. Here, she is vibrant, with maximum intensity and minimum fuss, as a Second World War wife whose husband is missing in action and who takes up with another man, to mixed reactions from her friends. She and Laurie Paton join the long line of Canadian actresses who have given outstanding performances in John Murrell's 1977 play about five Calgary women doing their bit on the home front. (Royal George Theatre, Niagara-on-the-Lake. Runs to Oct. 9. 1-800-511-7429. www.shawfest.com.)
