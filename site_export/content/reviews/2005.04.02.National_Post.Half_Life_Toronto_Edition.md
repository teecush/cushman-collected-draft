Tarragon Theatre - 3o Bridgman Ave. - 416-531-1827

John Mighton's play, a beauty in every department, is set in an old people's home, and is about memory, love and the quotidian reality of life within and beyond an institution. It's also a deft piece of storytelling. Daniel Brooks's direction is as luminous and tactful as the writing; so are the performances, with Carolyn Hetherington and Diego Matamoros wonderful as mother and son. Both subtle and satisfying: a permanent play about evanescence. Ends tomorrow.
