Pinter & Pirandello - Artword Theatre - 75 Portland St. - 416- 366-7723

A double bill of duologues, linking themes of jealousy, regret and sexual fantasy. Harold Pinter's The Lover, an old TV play about marriage, depends heavily on a surprise revelation that, in Levon Haftvan's production, is comprehensively muffed. Actors Brenda Bazinet and John Evans make even less of Pirandello's I'm Dreaming, But Am I?. It's always a bad sign when audiences don't realize a play is over. Here it happens twice. Ends tomorrow.
