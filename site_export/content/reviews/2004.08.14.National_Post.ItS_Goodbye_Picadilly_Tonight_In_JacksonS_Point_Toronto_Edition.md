The title of Douglas Bowie's entertaining and touching domestic drama is misleading; the culture clash it depicts is not primarily national, even though half the characters are old-money Canadian and the other half poor English. A public benefactor dies and his two families -- one legal, the other not -- do battle over his memory and the rural inn he used to own. (Red Barn Theatre, Jackson's Point. Closes tonight at 8 p.m. 905-722-3249).
