This year's Ross Petty pantomime is a good buy. Aladdin, which English panto always set in a music-hall Imperial China, here returns to a kind of Middle East, though with funky music and Maritime comic accents. Ted Dykstra's direction is, by genre standards, ferociously disciplined. Adam Brazier is a plucky hero and Jennifer Dale does a star turn as a librarian turned gyrating Genie. The other Genie is Bret "Hitman" Hart in an acting debut as impressive as any ex-wrestler is ever likely to make. (Elgin Theatre, ends tonight. 416-314-2901.)
