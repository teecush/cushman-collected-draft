An adaptation of Alistair Macleods novel in which the narrator (R. H. Thomson) does most of the talking, though the crucial character in No Great Mischief is his brother (David Fox). Maybe its meant to be about their whole family, descendants of the clan Macdonald who came from Scotland to Cape Breton in the 18th century, but there's no time for that. The problem of displaced focus is compounded by the curse of Canadian content, complete with Cape Breton dancing and fiddling. (Tarragon Theatre, until Dec. 12. 416- 531-1827.)
