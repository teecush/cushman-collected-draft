The Little Dragon of Keira Loughran's play is a first-year Queen's University student who thinks she may be the illegitimate daughter of Bruce Lee, and the production uses martial arts (all helpfully differentiated) as an ongoing metaphor for psychological conflicts. This is ingenious but mannered. The campus romping is lively but unfocused, the main issue contrived. Loughran plays her own protagonist, compellingly; Michelle Polak gives fierce support. (Theatre Passe Muraille, until Jan. 30, 416-504-7529.) R.C.
