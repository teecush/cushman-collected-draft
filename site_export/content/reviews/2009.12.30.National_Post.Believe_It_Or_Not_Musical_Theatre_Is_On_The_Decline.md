Further to the decline of literacy: commercially successful musicals of the past decade have fallen into three categories:

-Jukebox musicals (no, I dont like the phrase either), meaning assemblages of preexisting pop hits, with no inherent dramatic presence.

-Slavish adaptations of pre-sold properties, not all of them movies (though they're the most offensive, because they allow the least alteration). The giveaway is that they never change the title.

Musicals, like opera and Shakespeare, have always thrived on adaptation, but just as Shakespeare wrote As You Like It, not Rosalynde: The Comedie, so Lerner and Loewe wrote My Fair Lady, not Pygmalion: The Musical. (I seem to have that play on the brain.)

-Worse than either of the above, which are at least up-front about their intentions: musicals that treat the mere idea of doing a musical as an outrageous giggle in itself. When everything's a parody, whats left to be funny about? Oh Irony, what crimes are committed in thy name.

These categories sometimes overlap, though in all honesty I cant think of a show that falls into all three of them. Probably Mamma Mia! comes closest.

This doesn't mean that intelligent, musically adventurous, and even witty shows aren't being written. Just don't expect to see them on a big stage.
