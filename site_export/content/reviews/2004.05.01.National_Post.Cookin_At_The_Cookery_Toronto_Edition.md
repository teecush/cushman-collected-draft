Jackie Richardson as the jazz, blues and cabaret singer Alberta Hunter and Montego Gover as everyone else make this an enjoyable evening. But the show retains its stunning indifference to (and ignorance of) the facts of Hunter's life and music, from the Chicago clubland of the teens and '20s to her octogenarian comeback in the '70s. The guiding principle seems to be that any old song will do, while the non-musical scenes are crummy. (New Yorker Theatre, 651 Yonge St., 416-872-1111. Until June 6.)
