Richard Greenberg's title is, in baseball terms, a triple play -- on words: the game itself, a star player coming out as gay, slaughter on the diamond. This U.S. prize-winner has drive and humour, but is also a bit glib and brittle, and its real subject is elusive. Mainly it's a celebration: Let's play ball. Excellent direction (Morris Panych), design (Ken MacDonald) and performances: David Storch is brilliant and Mike Shara electrifying. Until Feb. 12. R.C.
