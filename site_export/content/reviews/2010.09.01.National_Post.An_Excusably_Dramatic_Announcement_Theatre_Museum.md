Theatre impresario David Mirvish announced on Monday that Theatre Museum Canada will finally have a permanent home in a new development at 355 King St. W., in the heart of Torontos Mirvish-controlled theatre district.

Planning is underway for a museum of 900 square metres that will include space for exhibits, presentation and theatrical programming.

Up until now the museum, which was registered as a charity in 1991 by its founding chairman, veteran theatre critic Herbert Whittaker, has led a nomadic existence, mounting exhibits at the Elgin and Wintergarden Theatre Centre, the Toronto Reference Library and other places.

Its programs have included Backstage, a series of educational theatre discussions, and an online history project, the Legend Library, in which R. H. Thomson has interviewed such seminal theatrical figures as William Hutt, Martha Henry and Paul Thompson. (The videos can be found at TheatreMuseumCanada. ca.)

The new museum was announced at a celebration in the Toronto home of board member Nona Macdonald Heaslip. Honorary patron Christopher Plummer sent a message proclaiming "God bless David Mirvish, everyone responsible and 'all who sail in her' for magically evoking this major miracle."
