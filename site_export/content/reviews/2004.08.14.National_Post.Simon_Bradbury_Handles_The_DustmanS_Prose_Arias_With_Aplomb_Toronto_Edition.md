A treat. Shaw's comedy Pygmalion, an effortlessly witty hard- edged romance, is given a panoramic production, splendiferously designed, with Jim Mezon and Tara Rosling charting a clear and exhilarating line through the relationship of the professor and the flower girl, and Simon Bradbury delivering dustman Doolittle's prose arias with superb aplomb. My Fair Lady hasn't killed this play off. There's room for both, and one wouldn't want to be without either. (Festival Theatre, Niagara-on-the-Lake, 1-800-511-7429. Runs until November.)
