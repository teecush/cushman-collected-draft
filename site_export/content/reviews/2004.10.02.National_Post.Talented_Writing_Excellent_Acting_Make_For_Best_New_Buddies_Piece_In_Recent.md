In Snowman, actor-playwright Greg MacArthur has written an unsatisfying but resundingly talented piece, the best new work at Buddies in recent memory. Four lonely or transient people try and fail to connect in a small sub-Arctic township. Their monologues have a distinctive voice, sawn-off and weather-beaten, but the result is less a play than a dramatized novella. The acting is excellent, with the usually elegant Philippa Domville unrecognizeably transformed in curlers and housecoat. (Buddies in Bad Times Theatre, until Oct. 12. 416-975-8555.)
