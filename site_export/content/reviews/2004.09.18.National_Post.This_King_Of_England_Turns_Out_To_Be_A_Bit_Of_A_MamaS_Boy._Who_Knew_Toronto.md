An intelligently ambiguous production (by Antoni Cimilino) of a play full of powerful scenes that don't quite cohere. People continually break faith or change sides. John himself (a mama's boy, as played by Stephen Ouimette) is a virtuoso of deceit, but not a very dynamic one. Jonathan Goad, who gets better and better, is a vigorous Bastard, Diane D'Aquila a Constance heartbreakingly logical in madness and Tom McCamus a wall-eyed Hubert, wrenchingly transformed. (Tom Patterson Theatre, Stratford. Runs until Sept. 24. 800-567-1600.)
