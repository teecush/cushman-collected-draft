The best musical here since who-knows-when: a through-sung two- hander about two young New Yorkers who meet, marry and bleakly part. His story, told chronologically, alternates with hers, told in reverse; they meet only at the midpoint, their wedding -- an apt metaphor for the relationship of two people for whom love just isn't enough. Jason Robert Brown's lyrics are very funny; his music is the first contemporary pop score to work dramatically. (Berkeley Street Theatre, 26 Berkeley St., 416-368-3110. Until May 29.)
