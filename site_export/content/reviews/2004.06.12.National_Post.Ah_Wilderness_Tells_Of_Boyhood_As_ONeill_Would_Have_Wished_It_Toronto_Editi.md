Eugene O'Neills one comedy, a gentle one, is a nostalgic piece, recalling family life in "a large small town" in 1904 Connecticut -- ONeill's boyhood as he would have liked it to have been. His wish- fulfilling dream had, by the end of the Depression, become all of America's. The implicit contrast between fantasy and reality makes the play poignant as well as charming and funny. Joseph Ziegler's production is lovely, with fine work from Norman Browning, as the father. (Courthouse Theatre, Niagara on-the-Lake, 1-800-511-7429)
