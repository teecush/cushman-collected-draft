Two suburban couples (of the men, one is a Richard and the other is a Dick) reveal themselves at a dinner party as a collection of unhappy breeders festering in slime. As an arraignment of society it's like Who's Afraid of Virginia Woolf, only nastier, and with the guests, rather than the hosts, as the aggressors. Gord Rand's Pond Life sustains an insouciantly bleak comic tone with uncommon success, and his direction is even more assured than his script. The four actors are perfectly in style. (Factory Studio Theatre, until Dec. 18. 416-504-9971.)
