In Trout Stanley, Claudia Dey delivers a masterwork. Glamorous Grace and quietly desperate Sugar, 30-year old twin sisters, run the town garbage dump and manufacture tragic figurines, respectively. This, without a hint of camp, is a play about people who live at extremes of climate and feeling, joyously written with tense, witty verbal duels and soaring prose arias. Unimprovably acted by Melody A. Johnson, Michelle Giroux and Gord Rand as the titular intruder, who is not a fish. (Factory Theatre, until Feb 8, 416-504-9971.)
