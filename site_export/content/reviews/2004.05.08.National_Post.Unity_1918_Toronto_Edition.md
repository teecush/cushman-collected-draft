Unity, Sask., is the site of Kevin Kerr's play about an influenza epidemic that took millions of lives just as the First World War was ending. The play is an unfocused fantasia on the subject of sex (frustrated) and death (achieved); some variations on the theme are, so to speak, left over from the war. Despite a few sweet or piercing moments, the fictitious characters are so many marionettes, all awaiting their turn to die or mourn. Theatre Passe Muraille Backspace, 16 Ryerson Ave., 416-504-7529. Runs until May 16.
