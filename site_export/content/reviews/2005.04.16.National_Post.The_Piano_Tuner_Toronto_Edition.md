Cameron House - 408 Queen St. W. - 416-703-1725

Robert Astle's self-performed monologue is so wispy it barely exists. A blind piano tuner fishes around in the interstices of his instrument, and finds objects that remind him of his unhappy past life. If this story -- a melodramatic one -- is worth dramatizing, then why not dramatize it, instead of just telling us about it? The acting is as tentative and unformed as the writing. Closes tomorrow.
