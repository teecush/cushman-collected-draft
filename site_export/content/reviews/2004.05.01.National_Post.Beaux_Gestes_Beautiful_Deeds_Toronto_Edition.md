A barely dramatized memoir -- one of those plays in which the characters' main claim on our attention is that they are related to the author. Marie-Lynn Hammond stands centre-stage and recalls her two Montreal grandmothers: Anglo society gal Elsie and poor Catholic mother-of-10 Corinne. Parochial, and linked by whiny, finger- wagging songs, but with excellent performances by Maria Ricossa as Elsie and especially Deborah Lalonde-Grover as Corrine. (Artword Theatre, 75 Portland St., 416-366-7723. Until May 9.)
