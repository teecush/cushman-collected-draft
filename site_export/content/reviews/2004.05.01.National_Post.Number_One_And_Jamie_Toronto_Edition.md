In Paula Wing's play, Jamie is a 10-year-old whose problem, like most people's, is fear. His parents are constantly splitting up, and menace lurks in the corners of his apartment building -- menace puzzlingly but effectively represented by skulking figures out of film noir. The wisecracking, wheelchair-bound landlady recruits Jamie for her private espionage schemes: She's frightened, too. A compelling, intelligent play and production. (Lorraine Kimsa Theatre for Young People, 165 Front St. E., 416-862-2222. Until May 9.)
