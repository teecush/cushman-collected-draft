The familiar VideoCabaret Canadian-history style (short scenes, fierce lighting, dazzling quick changes by the actors) remains the same in Deanne Taylor's play about the attempted selling-out of "a lakeside metropolis," but the material is coy, declining to name real names. Anand Rajaram is sublime, though, as a mayor with a chronic foot-in-mouth condition and horrendous hair. (Cameron House, 408 Queen St. W., 416-703-1725. Runs until May 30.)
