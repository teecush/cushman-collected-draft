Haunted Masters is a Halloween treat (not trick): four ghost stories, adapted from famous writers (Dickens, James, Lawrence, Wharton) played out in various rooms of a house that has its own claims to being haunted. None of the pieces plays like an adaptation. Each moves fluently from scene to scene. The production, with four actors, is weak on period style but strong on murky atmospherics. (Spadina Museum, until Nov. 6. 416-392-6910.)
