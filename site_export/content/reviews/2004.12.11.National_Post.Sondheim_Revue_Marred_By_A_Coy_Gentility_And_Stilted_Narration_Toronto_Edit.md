Stephen Sondheim has become a cultural icon, so Side by Side by Sondheim, a proselytizing 1976 revue, may have have outlived its historical purpose. It could still be a decent evening's entertainment, but this production is loaded with handicaps, including a coy gentility of approach inherent in the piece itself. The four actors look uncomfortable trying to pass off the original deadpan narration as spontaneous banter. An evening like this should leave you wanting more. This one seems long. (Bluma Appel Theatre, until Dec. 18. 416-366-7723.) R.C.
