This Korean musical is inoffensive but inert. It's an instructive history lesson, though, about Korea's 19th-century choices between tradition and modernity, the latter represented by an aggressive Japan whose samurai commandos eventually kill the Evita-like heroine. The show looks impressive, though its choreography suggests that Korean dance and the routines at Radio City Music Hall sprang from a common source. The score is imitation Euro-American pop- opera. (Hummingbird Centre, 416-872-2262. Runs until Sept. 1.)
