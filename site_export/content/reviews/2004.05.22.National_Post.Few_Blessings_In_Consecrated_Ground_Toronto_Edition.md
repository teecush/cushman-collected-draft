Obsidian Theatre's production does no favours to George Boyd's play, Consecrated Ground, about the governmental expropriation of Africville, a Halifax slum-ghetto, in 1965. The author's indignation sets off some sparks, but the action halts and hops from one scene to another. The direction is limp, and Kevin Hanchard is the only actor to play with consistent force and fluency. (Factory Theatre, 125 Bathurst St., 416-504-9971. Runs until May 30.)
