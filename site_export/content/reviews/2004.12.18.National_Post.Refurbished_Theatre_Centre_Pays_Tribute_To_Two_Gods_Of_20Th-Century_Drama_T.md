A refurbished theatre pays tribute to the two gods of 20th- century drama. Samuel Beckett is represented by That Time, an assemblage of five plays that take a disparaging but strangely cheering view of the human condition. Excellent, faithful acting and direction. Then comes Bebe, about Brecht and his women, to whom he was a bastard. The history is interesting, the drama fragmentary and the style imitation Berlin cabaret, of which enough already. (Theatre Centre, ends tomorrow. 416-538-0988.)
