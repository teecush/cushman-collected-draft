Royal George Theatre - Niagara-on-the-Lake - 1-800-511-7429

Somerset Maugham's 1926 play is dated (married career women are no longer novelties) but in a stimulating way. Laurie Paton plays a London surgeons wife who comes to view herself as "a prostitute who doesnt deliver the goods"; so she winks at her husband's adultery, while taking on a job and a lover of her own. Laurie Paton as the constant Constance is devastating and disarming, though she smiles too much. Otherwise Neil Munro's production is excellent. Through Oct. 9.
