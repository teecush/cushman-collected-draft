Brian Friel's play is about the political power of language, not just in making the rules but in defining the territory. Into 1820s rural Ireland comes the English army to anglicize the local place names. It's a political play, a love story and many other things, though the action and characters trail off toward the end. Especially good Soulpepper performances by Diego Matamoros, David Storch, Patricia Fagan and Gordon Rand. (Premiere Dance Theatre, 235 Queens Quay W. Runs until Aug. 14. 416-973-4000).
