The ultimate revenge fantasy, Dumas's The Count of Monte Cristo is a complicated story with a very simple thrust. The complications, though, are a large part of the point -- one thats missed by this two-hour adaptation, which scoops up every cliche that offers itself. The protagonists role is inexplicably divided between a young and an older actor. The hydraulic aspects of the staging are impressive, but it's a dog of a script. (Avon Theatre, Stratford, 1- 800-567-1600 or visit stratfordfestival.ca)
