CRITIC'S PICKTHEATREThree winners: There is everything to celebrate about A Whistle in the Dark (Berkeley Street Upstairs; 416-368-3110), but the greatest thrill is to see Joseph Ziegler, playing the paterfamilias of a brawling brood, turn before your eyes from an excellent actor to a great one. As he stands on a chair at the play's terrible climax, he sums up a whole complex character, a pitiable blusterer with a poetic streak who has raised evasion to an art form. This Anglo-Irish play, 44 years old and worth the wait, is an early work by the veteran Tom Murphy. The direction, by visiting Hibernian Jason Byrne, and the other seven performances are only perfect. The Bear (The Grange Room at AGO; 416-979-6608) is an adventurous site- specific production of a Chekhov farce, hilarious and also subtle, with David Ferry at top speed, finding the nuances in a more sympathetic blowhard.
