Both Soulpepper double-bill productions go down very well. In Pinter's The Dumb Waiter, Michael Hanrahan and Stuart Hughes provide two modes of humour, two modes of tension, and this Soulpepper production plays them to the hilt. The same two actors reappear in The Zoo Story. Hughes as the urban outcast finds a hypnotic voice for his big monologue. Hanrahan gives a beautifully complete account of the publisher on whom he battens. (Harbourfront Centre Theatre, 235 Queens Quay W. Until Aug. 17. 416-973-4000.)Word count: 93
