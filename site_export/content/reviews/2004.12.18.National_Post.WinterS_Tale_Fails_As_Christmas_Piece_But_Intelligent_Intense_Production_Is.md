Theatre Kingston attempts to turn The Winters Tale, one of Shakespeares last plays, full of resurrection and reconciliation, into a Christmas piece. It doesn't work, but the production is a good one: intelligent, intense and intimate. Matthew Gibson's jealous Leontes is superb, paranoia incarnate, and in full command of a notoriously treacherous text. Other performances are less accomplished, and the pastoral scenes flop, but the magical last scene induces the old ancestral shiver. (Brigantine Room.Harbourfront. Ends Dec. 23. 416-973-4000.)
