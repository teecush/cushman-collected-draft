CRITIC'S PICK

All the citys a stage

And thick and fast they come at last ... Toronto is once again awash with shows. We are finally getting Edward Albees Goat (or Who Is Sylvia) in a production by CanStage at their Berkeley Street facility. At the mainstage Bluma Appel, the same company offers their annual Jackie Richardson vehicle, directed as ever by Marion J. Caffey; this one is called Crowns. The Stage 3 season at Theatre Passe Muraille moves into the Israeli-Palestinian conflict with an explosive quasi-monologue called Territories. And the Tarragon crosses new horizons of scenic realism in Morris Panych's The Dishwashers, featuring Randy Hughson, who was trimphant in Panych's Earshot (which hell shortly be reprising).
