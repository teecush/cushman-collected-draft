CRITIC'S PICK

HELLO DOLLY, SO LONG STRATFORD

This weekend sees the closing of all but one of the Stratford Festival's main stage productions. Pride of place has to go to The Tempest in which, on Friday, the magnificent William Hutt as Prospero gives his farewell performance. Hutt has emphatically stated that this signals his departure from the stage, not necessarily from acting, and that he has no intention of shedding last-night tears. Saturday sees the closure of the much-underrated Cat on a Hot Tin Roof, The Lark and Fallen Angels. On Sunday it's curtains (or would be if they had them) for As You Like It and the great musical Into the Woods. Only Hello Dolly remains, for one more week.
