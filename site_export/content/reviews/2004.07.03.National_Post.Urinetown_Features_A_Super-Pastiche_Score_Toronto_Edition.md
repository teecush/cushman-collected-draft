All toilet facilities have been handed over to a private corporation that runs them on a fee-for-use basis. A musical satire on matters political and environmental that's also a satire on the idea of doing a musical satire in the first place. It's not always as funny as it thinks it is but certainly as clever. The score is a super-pastiche that retains its own delighted voice; the staging adds its own zany commentary; and the first 10 minutes are musical- comedy heaven. Runs until Labour Day weekend. (Bluma Appel Theatre, 27 Front St. E., 416-368-3110.)
