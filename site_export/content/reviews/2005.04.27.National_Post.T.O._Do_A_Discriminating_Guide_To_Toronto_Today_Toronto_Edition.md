4. CRITICS' PICK

THEATRE

This is the last week of the WorldStage Flying Solo season at Harbourfront. Caroline OConnor, the Anglo-Australian musical- comedy diva, proves herself as a dramatic, in a set of four monologues under the general title Bombshells. From closer to home: Denise Clarke, of Calgarys terrific One Yellow Rabbit troupe, has a show courageously titled A Fabulous Disaster. From yellow rabbits (or one of them) to hip hop seems a reasonable association of ideas; Beji Reid, described as freestylin' physical-theatre breakdancer" (sounds very energetic) "questions" the genre in 13 Mics and Style 4 Free. Charlie Ross, known from the Toronto fringe festival, has a one-man Star Wars AND a one-man Lord of the Rings: one hopes that he is auditioning for every single role in the upcoming musical of the latter. Back on the beaten track: CanStage's Aint Misbehavin is the opposite of a one-man show, an ensemble piece in which every member of the six-strong cast gets to grab a piece of Fats Waller -- and of course there's plenty to grab. Apart from the obvious hits, songs range from the sublime Jitterbug Waltz to the funny Ladies Who Sing With the Band. Also from CanStage, by way of the NAC: Trying, by the well-thought-of Joanna Glass, acted by the well-thought-of Caroline Cave and Paul Soles. That opens this week, as does Alice's Affair, the new play from Susan Coyne with a distinctly Soulpeppery cast.

HOT DOCS FILM FESTIVAL

In Lifelike, Montreal director Tally Abecassis casts a loving lens on the little-known and out-of-favour subculture of taxidermy, interviewing old pros and novices in the art of making dead animals seem alive. While the participation of several wildlife artists in a competition in Orillia, Ont., provides the through-line for this short and sweet film, Abecassis also profiles some out-of- competition enthusiasts, including a charming French-Canadian hunter who cut a hole in his ceiling for his giant stuffed giraffe. The sub- plot of a woman who, inspired by an episode of Lifestyles of the Rich and Famous, has her beloved dead dog Wonder mounted is hilarious and then unsuspectingly touching. Like the characters in a Christopher Guest mockumentary, you might laugh at Abecassis taxidermists at first, but they quickly win you over and you start to root for them and their off-beat yet age-old pursuit. HHH Screens tonight at 9:30 p.m. at the Bloor Cinema and Friday at 7:15 p.m. at the Royal Ontario Museum.
