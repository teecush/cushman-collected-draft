The great triumph of this production is that Peter Donaldson's Timon, the prodigal misanthrope, is believably the same man throughout. There is a force and sternness to his early benevolence, and a superb vein of gnarled humour in his later tirades. Stephen Ouimette's modern-dress production works brilliantly, especially in the first satiric half, which might have been written yesterday. By academic standards, Timon may be a bad play, but it's a great bad play. (Tom Patterson Theatre, Stratford, Ont. Runs to Sept. 25. 1- 800-567-1600).
