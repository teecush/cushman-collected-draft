In pppeeeaaaccceee, three actors talk -- mainly among themselves - - about life Before, During and After the Revolution, the last being axiomatically the best. Whimsy, a weaker version of irony, permeates the evening. The whole is framed as "an imaginary group hug." The actors tell us to close our eyes. Don't do it: The visual aspect of Darren O'Donnell's production (he's also the author) is its strongest point. (Theatre Passe Muraille, runs until Oct. 10. 416- 504-7529.)
