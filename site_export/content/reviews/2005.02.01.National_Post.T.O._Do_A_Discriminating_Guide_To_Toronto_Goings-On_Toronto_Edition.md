3. CRITICS PICKS

THEATRE

Now, in the calm after this year's initial storm of Toronto openings, may be the time to look ahead to April and the start of the Stratford and Shaw festivals, when at the very least the weather should be better. The only part of the repertoire to which both organizations habitually stake a claim is that of modern American classics, musical and other. Looking at the other, the dramas: Each festival offers two of them; all four open in June. The Shaw (905- 468-2172; www.shawfest.com) proposes Lillian Hellman's little-seen The Autumn Garden (1951), which is about mature love in Louisiana, and whose distinguished players include Jim Mezon, Goldie Semple, Peter Hutt, Sharry Flett and Patricia Hamilton; Martha Henry directs. William Inges Bus Stop (1955) is known to some as a stage vehicle for Kim Stanley, and to many more as a film vehicle for Marilyn Monroe; Nicole Underhay steps into their shoes and whatever else as the club singer marooned with her fellow travellers in a winter storm. Stratford (1-800-567-1600. www.stratfordfestival.ca) devotes itself in this area exclusively to Tennessee Williams. Richard Monette directs Cat on a Hot Tin Roof, Williams last really famous play, with Cynthia Dale as Maggie the Cat, David Snelgrove as her husband, Brick, and James Blendick as Big Daddy, a role he reprises from the last Stratford production. Sweet Bird of Youth is directed by Miles Potter with a formidable-looking cast: Seana McKenna, Jonathan Goad (currently wowing 'em in A Whistle in the Dark), Michelle Giroux (ditto in Trout Stanley), Scott Wentworth, Dixie Seatle and -- making a long-awaited Stratford return -- Fiona Reid.

UNLESS

Tickets are on sale for CanStage's production of Unless, by Carol Shields (author of Thirteen Hands, The Stone Diaries and Larrys Party). A woman with her life together suddenly finds her eldest daughter is falling apart -- she has quit university and the family and now sits on a street corner uncommunicative but for a sign around her neck that says Goodness." Directed by Roy Surette. Starring Nicola Cavendish, Tara Hughes, Nicola Lipman, Matthew MacFadzean, Allan Morgan, Elizabeth Saunders, Michael Spencer-Davis and Celine Stubel. Previews Feb. 21-23, regular run Feb. 24-March 19 at CanStage-Bluma. $36-$80. Student rates available. At 26 Berkeley St. or 27 Front St. E. box offices, Ticketmaster, 416-368-3110.

THEATRE

TAKE ME OUT

Richard Greenbergs Take Me Out is showing now at CanStage Theatre. The play tells the story of a rich, handsome baseball player who "outs" himself at the height of his career. Runs until Feb. 12. Monday to Saturday 8 p.m., Wednesday matinees, 1:30 p.m. and Saturday matinees, 2 p.m. 27 Front St. E. $36 to $80. 416-368- 3110. www.canstage.com.

TROUT STANLEY

Final week: Factory Theatre presents the Toronto premiere of a tragi-comic tale about a travel-worn stranger who may be connected to the disappearance of a local stripper and Scrabble champion. Runs to Feb. 6. Tuesday to Saturday 8 p.m., Sunday 2 p.m. (PWYC). Special Saturday matinee Feb. 5 at 2 p.m. $20-$34. Factory Theatre Mainspace, 125 Bathurst St. 416-504-9971. www.factorytheatre.com.

THE GLASS MENAGERIE

Tennessee Williams' classic opens tonight to previews at CanStage. This semi-autobiographical play explores a woman's dark and bewildering family as it gambles its future happiness on a mysterious gentleman caller. Runs to Feb. 26. Monday to Saturday 8 p.m., Wednesday 1:30 p.m., Saturday 2 p.m. $27 to $51. CanStage, 26 Berkeley St. 416-368-3110. www.canstage.com.

THE BROTHERHOOD OF MANNS

Final week: Alliesallover Theatre presents The Brotherhood of Manns, a dark comedy about the bonds of brotherhood. Runs to Feb. 5 at the Alchemy Theatre, 133 Tecumseth St. Wednesday through Sunday, 8 p.m, with matinees on Saturday, Sunday and Feb. 4 to 5 at 2 p.m. $10. 416-836-0289.

DA KINK IN MY HAIR

Written by Trey Anthony and directed by Weyni Mengesha, da Kink in my Hair is a musical that tells the stories of six black women living in Toronto, all linked to the same West Indian hair salon. Runs to Feb. 27, $25-65. Tuesday to Saturday 8 p.m., Wednesday and weekends 2 p.m. Princess of Wales Theatre, 300 King St. W. 416-872- 1212.
