Cul-de Sac shows Daniel MacIvor at the peak of his performing form. In this self-written solo show he plays Leonard, a gay man at the end of a dead-end street (literally) who is a magnificent creation, a gushing fountain of witty self-deprecation. He gives a second astonishing performance as Leonard's nemesis. The other characters, Leonard's neighbours, are a lot less interesting. The technical effects in Daniel Brooks's production are stupendous. (Buddies in Bad Times Theatre. Ends tomorrow. 416-975-8555.)
