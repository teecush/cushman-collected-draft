Marivaux's 1732 comedy, a masterpiece of understated irony, is given a production by Richard Monette that takes some tumbles on its way (too many of them literal) but ends up in the right place. Transvestite disguises and a Neverland setting may remind you of Shakespeare. Claire Jullien gives her best performance to date as a remarkably ruthless Leontine wooing a prince, his philosopher guardian and the guardian's bluestocking sister. (Studio Theatre, Stratford. 1-800-567-1600. stratfordfestival.ca. Runs until Sept. 25.)
