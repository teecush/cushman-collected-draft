Final weekend. An instant classic by Nigerian laureate Wole Soyinka. Yoruba custom dictates that the king's horseman follow his late master in an act of ritual suicide. He's a man whom fate has cast in a tragedy but who often seems to feel he would be more at home in a comedy. The cultural clashes, over two modes of life and two views of death, could hardly be more topical. The play has greatness in it. The less said about the production, the better. (Artword Theatre, 75 Portland St., 416-408-2783. Run ends tomorrow.)
