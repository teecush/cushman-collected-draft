The Stratford platform is a natural for Shakespeare's histories, and Richard Monette's production of this atypical one (no battles, just processions) is a tingling reminder of what we have been missing. Big lively stage pictures balance big lonely solo performances: Graham Abbey's golden-boy king, Walter Borden's effortlessly confident Wolsey and Seana McKenna's angry, sublime Queen Katharine. (Festival Theatre, Stratford. 800-567-1600, stratfordfestival.ca. Runs until Oct. 29.)
