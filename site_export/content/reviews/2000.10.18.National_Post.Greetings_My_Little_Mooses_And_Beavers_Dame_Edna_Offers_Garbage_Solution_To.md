"I don't do stand-up," said the Dame. "I'm a sit-down sort of person. I'm sitting down now. Of course I'm standing up, so that you can see me. But really I'm sitting down."

Dame Edna Everage, whose interactive theatrical experience, Dame Edna: The Royal Tour, opens at the Pantages Theatre on Nov. 7, was addressing the Toronto media at, fittingly, the Royal York Hotel yesterday.

Cameramen crammed the front rows, which is why she had to remain erect to be visible to the rest of us. She was worth seeing, attired in a lipstick-red dress, slit to reveal an impressive length of elegant leg. Her hair was its accustomed shade of electric mauve (natural wisteria, she calls it) and she wore her trademark rhinestone-encrusted spectacles.

Conspicuously absent from Dame Edna's entourage was Barry Humphries, the man she refers to as her manager. Asked about his responsibilities, she replied that his principal one seemed to be "to take an extortionate percentage of what I earn."

It is a suspicious fact that the two of them have never once been seen together in public.

She believes her Commonwealth roots (she is Australian) have given her an instinctive sympathy with the Dominion of Canada ("I love that word Dominion -- I think I'm a Dominionatrix"). She even speaks with sympathy about Toronto's garbage problem: She thinks we should compress it, send it to the Middle East, and use it to build a wall between the Israelis and the Palestinians.

She also speaks kindly, and with the brisk and generous candour that has always distinguished her, of her Toronto producers Ed and David Mirvish. "They seem to be lovely people. Of course, I haven't seen much of them yet. They're probably ghastly when you get to know them."

She has already developed an enormous affection for her new Canadian fan base: or, as she prefers to call us, "my little mooses and beavers."
