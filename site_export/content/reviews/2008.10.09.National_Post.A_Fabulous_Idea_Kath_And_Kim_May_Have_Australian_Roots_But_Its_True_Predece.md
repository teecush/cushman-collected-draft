The world could use a good and silly new sitcom, and Kath and Kim will more than do. Its drawn preliminary comparisons to Absolutely Fabulous; it isn't on that level, but then few things are.

Like AbFab, Kath and Kim is about a fractious relationship between a mother and daughter. The British show gave us a ditzy mom and her straitlaced offspring. This American one (based on a hit Australian series, hence doubly derivative) gives us two generations both alike in fecklessness. They are in fact in competition, a conflict less rich in possibilities but holding up well over the first two episodes. How well it can sustain is another thing.

Kath (Molly Shannon) is the mother, in her forties, divorced and dating. Kim (Selma Blair) is the daughter, twentyish, just married and already wanting out. Kim, in the opening moments of the pilot, arrives on Kath's doorstep, claiming that her husband has told her to "do things." Sexual things? asks Kath, interested. No, says Kim, he told me to cook dinner. (It later transpires that hed requested her to pop something in the microwave.) This isnt Kim's idea of being a trophy wife, which is apparently what she thought she was. She's miffed too that Kath, who is intent on staying in shape, has changed what was Kims bedroom into a miniature gym, throwing out all Kims possessions, including her collection of Care Bears. Soon afterwards, Kim's husband, a meek young man named Craig, arrives to reclaim her. "I'm putting my foot down", he says, but spoils it by adding, Please? He then tries blackmail: "If I go through that door, you wont see me again." Kim points out that this is unlikely, as the door is glass.

Craig works at the mall, in an electrical appliance outlet. Phil, who is Kaths boyfriend-going-on-fiance and truly adores her, also works in the mall. He's the proprietor of Phil's Sandwich Outlet, and theres a plot complication when he spots Kath eating a wrap at a rival establishment. The last Australian thing I saw, the stage play The Blonde, the Brunette and the Vengeful Redhead, also concerned jealousy, indeed outright mayhem, in a mall. Message: If youre Down Under, stay out of food courts.

Anyway, it's all very fitting, as Kath and Kim seem to spend most of their lives in malls. (Neither seems to have a job.) They're aggressively superficial. They live to shop, and also to eat. And to be admired. Kim is well up, too, on celebrity lore, remarking that if Britney Spears and Melanie Griffith could leave abusive relationships, then so can she. (Question: When did celebrities and performers become synonymous? Even today, its still possible to be famous for other things.) Kath seems a trifle more, shall we say, ironic about the celeb thing, saying at one point that she doesnt intend to be Catherine Zeta-Jonesed, which is quite hard to say.

And it can get quite witty: When Kim looks up from her magazine to announce that the stars are holding a rally against global warming, Kath murmurs, "How brave." And this time she isn't being ironic.

The shared narcissistic values of Kath and Kim reinforce my own grizzled conviction that nothing has changed in popular culture since the '80s, bar the odd dance step. This, in any but the most obvious sense, reduces the comic potential. I probably shouldnt push the AbFab comparison too hard, but the point of that show was that the Jennifer Saunders character was continuing to peddle a myth of the 60s that was long past its sell-by date. There was almost a moral element to the conflict between her and her daughter. Here Kim seems to be combining the roles of both daughter and spaced-out best friend, Joanna Lumley and Julia Sawalha. And Ab-Fab even had a third generation, the grandmother, making it even richer. If Kath has a mother, we've yet to hear about her. Also, the men in Absolutely Fabulous were marginal. In Kath and Kim they're front and centre, with the men, unusually in comedy, rather too good for the women (though presumably dumb for caring about them). I have a feeling that there's a lot of romance in this show, and that we're going to see both Kath and Kim bouncing back and forth between partners: a series of returns that could rapidly prove diminishing. So far, however, so pretty good. - Kath and Kim debuts tonight at 8:30 on E! and NBC.
