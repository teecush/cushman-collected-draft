As Vladimir, Samuel Beckett's senior tramp, William Hutt putters around the stage with his usual superb physical grace and physical wit. He moves daintily, partly because hes feeling his way with an old mans caution, partly because he's putting tentative toes into a metaphysical void. Albert Schultz's Soulpepper production is good but not great, though it stands a chance, as the run progresses, of achieving greatness. (Premiere Dance Theatre, Harbourfront. Runs until Sept. 24. 416-974-4000).
