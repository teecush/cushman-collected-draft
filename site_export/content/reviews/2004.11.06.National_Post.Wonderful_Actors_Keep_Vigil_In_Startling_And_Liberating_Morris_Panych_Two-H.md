Vigil is Morris Panych's startling and liberating two-hander about a man called to the bedside of his mortally ill aunt who finds himself stuck with her for the best part of a year. He expects her to hurry up and die, and says so, repeatedly. A one-joke situation, but most of the restatements are very funny, and there's a second- act revelation that explodes nearly everything. The actors are two of the best: Martha Henry, wondrous in an almost silent role, and Brent Carver. (Bluma Appel Theatre, until Nov. 13, 416-368-3110.)
