Mel Brooks's film was a satire. His stage show is a celebration of the great gleaming entertainment machine that used to be the American musical. Susan Stromans direction keeps the show moving and breathing, and her comic choreography is her best. Brookss pastiche music is unassumingly accurate -- better, surprisingly, than his lyrics. The Canadian cast is top-notch. "If you've got it, flaunt it." They have, and they do, and we love it. (Canon Theatre, 244 Victoria St., 416-872-1212. Open run.)
