A fascinating evening and a heartening one, with as benevolent a bunch of characters as you could hope to encounter in the middle of the Second Crusade. G.E. Lessing's play is a comedy of good intentions that could as easily turn tragic. This Soulpepper production lacks danger but is good on argument. William Webster as a universally respected Jewish merchant radiates humour and, yes, wisdom; and there are other fine performances. Runs until July 31. (Harbourfront Theatre Centre. 235 Queens Quay W. 416-973-4000).
