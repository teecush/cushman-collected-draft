Royal George Theatre, Niagara on-the-Lake.

Pal Joey (1940) is the first realistic musical comedy, a pioneering show that never seems to work as well as one wants it to. Its score, by Rodgers and Harts exulted standards, is second-rate (though it does have Bewitched, in all its velvety candour) and none of the songs fits very well with John OHara's dialogue, which loses the narrating voice of the original stories. Still, Alisa Palmer's Shaw Festival production is solid; Adam Barzier's Joey is "fresh" in a double sense and Laurie Paton looks stunning and sings well.
