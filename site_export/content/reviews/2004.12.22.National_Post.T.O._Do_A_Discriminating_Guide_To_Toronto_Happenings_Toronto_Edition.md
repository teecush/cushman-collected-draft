CRITICS PICK

THEATRE

Kids who know Aladdin only through the Disney movie may be surprised to learn that the hero had a comic, widowed, cross- dressing mama and she had two funny, incompetent assistants. That may not be authentic Arabian Nights but it's pure English panto, as interpreted in Ross Petty's seasonal transplant, one of his best. Petty himself is, of course, the villain, and in the prologue a down- and-out sheltering in a library; he acts this well. Jennifer Dale is the very sexy librarian. Let us pause to consider this universal fantasy. Now let us move on. Adam Brazier is the charmingly gormless skateboarding hero, who rubs his lamp and gets a wrestler; thats Bret The Hitman" Hart making his acting debut as a genie. Robin Williams he isnt, but at least he's there in the flesh. Until Dec. 31 at the Elgin Theatre. For tickets call 416-872-5555.

THEATRE

BED & BREAKFAST

Puppetmongers present a tale set in an Edwardian-style dollhouse peopled with tiny perfect servants and aristocrats. Runs to Jan. 2, with no performances Dec. 24, 25 or 31. Monday to Saturday 2:30 p.m., plus Thursday evening 8 p.m. $14, Monday half-price. Tarragon Theatre, 30 Bridgman Ave. 416-531-1827. www.puppetmongers.com.

LEAVE THE PORCH LIGHT ON

Famous People Players presents a heartwarming holiday show featuring highlights from the Nutcracker, a mean tattooed pig running a diner, ostriches learning to fly, and more. Music written and composed by Doug Riley. Voices by Andrea Martin, Eugene Levy, Nathan Lane, Martin Short, Michael Burgess and Catherine O'Hara. Runs through December. Tuesday to Saturday. Dinner at 7 p.m., performance 8 p.m. $34.95-$48.95. Reservations and information 416- 532-1137. 110 Sudbury St. www.fpp.org.

THE MERMAID THEATRE

Tickets are on sale for Guess How Much I Love You, narrated by Beau Bridges. Presented by The Mermaid Theatre of Nova Scotia and featuring Little Nutbrown Hare and Big Nutbrown Hare. Runs Dec. 27- 31 at the Lorraine Kimsa Theatre for Young People. 1 p.m. and 3 p.m. 416-862-2222.
