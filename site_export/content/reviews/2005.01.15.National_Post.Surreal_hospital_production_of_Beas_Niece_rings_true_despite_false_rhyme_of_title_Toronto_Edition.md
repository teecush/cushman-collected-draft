David Gow's title, Bea's Niece, is a false rhyme, a false pun and a false pointer. Bea's niece, a novelist on a psychiatric ward, does have an aunt (a sort of rabinnical Auntie Mame) but she also has a shrink and an ex-husband, and her relationships with them are just as important. The play is diffuse with subjects and symbols dotted about the stage. Richard Rose's production looks good on a superreal hospital set. (Tarragon Theatre, until Feb. 6, 416-531-1827.)
