Rick Roberts's play offers variations on themes from Samuel Beckett. Man One and Man Two are both very old; One (a very fine Oliver Dennis) is flying a kite, which gets stuck in a tree; Two (a piping Marjorie Campbell) has an assignation with a younger woman, and would like One to leave. It's about the indignities of old age, taken to metaphorical extremes, and has some tedious passages and some with real resonance. (Tarragon Theatre Backspace, 30 Bridgeman Ave., 416-531-1827. Run ends tomorrow.)
