THEATRE REVIEW STUPIDHEAD! Theatre Passe Muraille

Stupidhead! is a small-scale musical: one writer-performer, one writer-accompanist.

The performer is Katherine Cullen, and the show is her autobiographical account of living with dyslexia: not the regular kind that affects reading but a variant that causes problems with numbers and with spatial recognition. So she gets lost in unfamiliar places, and even in familiar ones.

"This," she proclaims, "is a show about the glamour of failure." Actually it's a show that has its share of wry self-deprecation, but is more about living with and even overcoming obstacles. It's about following your dreams, even if they include starring in a musical despite having no musical training and, really, not that great a voice.

Cullen, though, is a witty and dynamic performer and in this show, a kind of victorious confessional, she makes erratic singing into a style. It helps, too, that she has some funny stories to tell. I'm not sure that she always tells them in the most effective order. She darts about, without concern for chronological order.

The show was seen in a shorter version at last year's Summerworks, and not all the additions are good ones. Her collaborator, Britta Johnson, wrote and plays the songs, and they're mostly a joy, full of ingeniously rhymed words that bounce delightfully off their accompanying notes. The best number, as before, is one about an unfortunate date with a fellow who had listed his interests online as Dobermans and Nutella: "one is vicious, the other delicious."

The show is nearly all delicious.
