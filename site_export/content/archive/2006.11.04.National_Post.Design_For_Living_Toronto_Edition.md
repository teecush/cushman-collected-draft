DESIGN FOR LIVING

Royal George Theatre Niagara-on-the-Lake

1-800-511-7429

Noel Coward wrote this menage-a-trois comedy as a vehicle for himself and his friends the Lunts, and it shows. Morris Panych's fine production, however, refuses to indulge the central threesome, losing a few laughs but gaining immensely in emotional reality. Nicole Underhays Gilda is a star performance without star tactics, a generous woman scared of whom she may be hurting; her partners, David Jansen and Graeme Somerville, are more mischievous, while Lorne Kennedy, the outsider, marvellously demonstrates the impotence of being Ernest. In rep through Nov. 18.

HIGH SOCIETY

Festival Theatre Niagara-on-the-Lake

-800-511-7429

The show of the movie musical of the play. It never worked, and this busy production is particularly coarse. The augmented Cole Porter score is made up not of songs the characters just have to sing but of songs its not quite impossible they might. Dan Chameroy and the sets are good. In rep through Nov. 19.

OF MICE AND MEN

Bluma Appel Theatre 27 Front St. E.

- 416-368-3110

John Steinbeck's classic novel-into-play is devilishly well constructed, and Dennis Garnhum's production is excellent, with magnificent sets, alternating stunning vistas of golden corn with timbered interiors. Shaun Smyth is an edgy George, Ashley Wright a trusting one-track Lennie, and theres a bunkhouseful of other good performances. The story shows the destruction of all best-laid plans, but play and production are proof that they can sometimes work out. Through Nov. 11.

WICKED

Canon Theatre 244 Victoria St.

- 416-872-1212

A sort of parallel text to The Wizard of Oz but one in which narrative consistency goes by the board, music and lyrics are bombastic apart from a couple of nice tunes, and sets and costumes are cluttered. Its preachy but doesn't have the guts to follow through to the logical unhappy ending. Ding dong, the wicked old witch is dead -- but this one isn't wicked, old or even dead -- just misunderstood. Through Dec. 31.

DEAD AIR!

Mysteriously Yours 2026 Yonge St.

- 416-486-7469

The plot and characters of the new Mysteriously Yours show are the same as the plot and characters of all their previous shows. But this time theres a new wrinkle; this, as its subtitle says, is a musical murder mystery that sharpens the element of parody, here directed at old-time network radio presenting cowboy stars in things with titles like Withering Heights. (A Brontesaurus?) The songs are neat and all the cast have voices, including Jean Daigle doing his customary interactive turn as both corpse and sleuth. Indefinite run.

- - -

APPLE

Factory Theatre 125 Bathurst St.

- 416-504-9971

At its core (ha!), Apple is not "a cancer play," though it treats that subject with forthrightness and dignity. It also uses it, not too successfully, as a metaphor for the destruction and regeneration of relationships. Its the domestic drama itself, pared down to extremes, that makes Vern Thiessen's play and Ken Gass's production so fine. As the trio of stricken wife, jobless husband, and mistress- doctor, Sarah Orenstein, Kevin Hanchard and Niki Landau are both not quite right and so good it doesnt matter. Through Nov. 12.

- - -

BIRD FLU OVER THE CUCKOOS NEST

Second City 51 Mercer St.

- 416-343-0011

The groan-title has a modicum of relevance; there is a number about avian flu, an East Indian calypso. Political satire fails, as usual, to materialize, but there are several goodies including Naomi Snieckus radiating every variety of shock and delight as a mother hacking into her son's MSN account. Indefinite run.
