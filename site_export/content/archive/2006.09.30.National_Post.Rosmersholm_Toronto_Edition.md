Court House Theatre

Niagara-on-the-Lake

A revelation. Neil Munro has updated Ibsen's showpiece of cat- and-mouse dramaturgy to between the wars. More important, he has eliminated white horses and other misty symbols, and made the people and the politics specific and passionate. A riveting trio of central performances: Waneta Storm's intent Rebecca, Patrick Galligan's open- faced Rosmer and Peter Hutt's magnificent Kroll subtly and speedily conveying extraordinary shades of feeling. In rep through Oct. 7.
