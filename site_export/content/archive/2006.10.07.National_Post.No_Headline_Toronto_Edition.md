I AM NOT A DINNER MINT Diesel Playhouse 56 Blue Jays Way - 416- 971-5656

The title, meant to be a female manifesto, is forced and far- fetched, and the subtitle -- "the crap that women swallow to stay in relationships" -- turns out to have meaninglessly wide application. However, the show's commitment to complaint doesn't stop it from being vivaciously cheerful, and a stark monologue by Laura Rickards (co-author, with Trey Anthony) is kink-in-my-hair-raising. Through Oct. 15.

HERE LIES HENRY Buddies in Bad Times Theatre 11 Alexander St. - 416-975-8555

The verb in the title is ambiguous. Henry, as written and played by Daniel MacIvor, is gay, lonely and angry. Or so he says, but then he also says he's a liar. Worrying about the veracity of a fictitious character in a one-man play seems kind of pointless, but MacIvor is such a spellbinding performer that it hardly matters; he fuses a battery of physical and vocal mannerisms into a coherent persona at lightning speed and with immaculate control. Through Oct. 15.

DESIGN FOR LIVING Royal George Theatre Niagara-on-the-Lake 1-800- 511-7429

Noel Coward wrote this menage a trois comedy as a vehicle for himself and his friends the Lunts, and it shows. Morris Panych's fine production, however, refuses to indulge the central threesome, losing a few laughs but gaining immensely in emotional reality. Nicole Underhay's Gilda is a star performance without star tactics, a generous woman scared of whom she may be hurting. Her partners, David Jansen and Graeme Somerville, are more mischievous, while Lorne Kennedy, the outsider, marvellously demonstrates the impotence of being Ernest. In rep through Nov. 18.

DEAD AIR! Mysteriously Yours 2026 Yonge St. - 416-486-7469

The plot and characters of the new Mysteriously Yours show are the same as the plot and characters of all their previous shows. But this time there's a new wrinkle; this, as its subtitle says, is a musical murder mystery that sharpens the element of parody, here directed at old-time network radio presenting cowboy stars in things with titles like Withering Heights. (A Brontesaurus?) The songs are neat and all the cast have voices, including Jean Daigle doing his customary interactive turn as both corpse and sleuth. Indefinite run.

HIGH SOCIETY Festival Theatre Niagara-on-the-Lake 1-800-511-7429

The show of the movie musical of the play (that was also a movie). It never worked, and this busy production is particularly coarse. The augmented Cole Porter score is made up not of songs the characters just have to sing but of songs it's not quite impossible they might; it's dehydrating. Dan Chameroy and the sets are good. In rep through Nov. 19.

THE MAGIC FIRE Court House Theatre Niagara-on-the-Lake 1-800-511- 7429

Lillian Groag has written a presumably autobiographical play about a young woman who, like herself, grew up in Peron's Argentina and moved to the United States. Her seven-year-old self is watched by her narrating self, which splits the focus. Her immigrant family (Austrian and Italian) fill up the stage, mostly talking about opera, their defence against the world about them. The point is made many times. Lively compensating performances by Jennifer Phipps, Michael Ball, Ric Reid and others. Ends tomorrow.

SOUTH PACIFIC Avon Theatre Stratford - 1-800-567-1600

Cynthia Dale's Nellie Forbush is her best performance, getting right inside her songs and making us feel her happiness. Director- choreographer Michael Lichtefeld keeps Rodgers and Hammerstein's most ostentatiously romantic musical crisp. From a distance it seems a stodgy show, with formulaic comedy, but in the performance we are conquered by its craft and awed by its self-belief. In rep through Oct. 28.

MUCH ADO ABOUT NOTHING Festival Theatre Stratford - 1-800-567- 1600

One of the least showy, but most humane and entertaining, of recent Stratford Shakespeares. Set in 1910 (as the program tells us, with unusual precision), it has a Benedick and Beatrice who play beautifully together: Peter Donaldson a bluff barker of a misogynist, with perfect throwaway timing, and Lucy Peacock a refugee from Bloomsbury, whose love is a refuge from anger. They come together when her fury is hottest, and it's very satisfying. In rep through Oct. 22.

THE CRUCIBLE Festival Theatre Niagara-on-the-Lake 1-800-511-7429

Tadeusz Bradecki delivers an exciting production of Arthur Miller's Salem witchcraft drama, a thrilling piece of storytelling with an earned moral fervour that is never likely to run short of contemporary applications. Benedict Campbell and Kelli Fox are fine as the persecuted Proctors and Jim Mezon terrifying as the Deputy Governor, a calm judicial mind turning itself into a one-man lynch mob. In rep through Oct. 14.

THE HEIRESS Royal George Theatre Niagara-on-the-Lake 1-800-511- 7429

Tara Rosling gives an extraordinary performance, rising to tragedy, as Henry James's shy, plain heroine. Mike Shara, smooth and gauche at the same time, is the suitor who, tantalizingly, may not be faking it. Taut direction by Joseph Ziegler, superb design by Christina Poddubiuk. Ends tonight.
