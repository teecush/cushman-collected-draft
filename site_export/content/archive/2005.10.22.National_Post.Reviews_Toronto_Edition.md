BETTER PARTS/FISH EYES

Theatre Passe Muraille Backspace 16 Ryerson Ave. - 416-504-8980

A pair of monologues, performed by their authors. Nicole Stamps Better Parts is a total joy, an office-temping actress's rhyming fantasies of being comparatively rich and famous, spoken to music: rap, but better. Anita Majumdar's Fish Eyes, about a girl who doesnt want to go to India for a dancing competition, is dexterously performed but lacks layers. In repertory through Oct. 29 and Nov. 5 respectively. R.C.

LES MISERABLES

Princess of Wales Theatre 300 King St. W. - 416-872-1212

"Jean Valjean, no evil-doer/Stole some bread cause he was po-er/ A detective chased him through a sewer/The End." Many prefer that Comden & Green precis of Les Mis to the later, longer Claude-Michel Schoenberg musical -- whose admirers can nevertheless catch it again, in all its grandiose banality, on what we are promised is its last tour. Through Nov. 5. R.C.

THE OPTIMISTS

Tarragon Theatre 30 Bridgman Ave. - 416-531-1827

Two couples -- one married, one engaged -- come to Las Vegas, though much of Morwyn Brebner's play might still have happened if they'd stayed in Canada. The characters are well-drawn, and well- acted, but their interaction seems random. Ends tomorrow. R.C.

BLUE MAN GROUP

Panasonic Theatre 651 Yonge St. - 416-872-1111

By turns charming, boring and annoying, the Blue Men specialize in percussion-pounding and juggling, with marshmallows and gumballs among other things. They also project a remarkable amount of personality, considering that their faces are entirely covered in blue masks. Indefinite run. R.C.

BUS STOP

Royal George Theatre Niagara-on-the-Lake - 1-800-511-7429

Of its kind, as good as it gets. William Inge's 1955 Broadway hit calls up memories of a tidier theatrical time. As the naive cowboy and the floosie-chantoosie, Martin Happer and Nicole Underhay bring two American archetypes to individual life. In repertory through Nov. 27. R.C.
