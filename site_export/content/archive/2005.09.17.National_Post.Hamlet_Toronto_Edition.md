Albert Schultz is an outstandingly lucid Hamlet, and an outstandingly likeable one. His soliloquies are unclouded windows into a mind that will consider any question and still end up dissatisfied. The Soulpepper ensemble, under Joseph Ziegler's direction, brings depth and clarity to the relationships, with William Webster a dazzling Polonius and Patricia Fagan's Ophelia her best performance yet. In repertory through Oct. 1.
