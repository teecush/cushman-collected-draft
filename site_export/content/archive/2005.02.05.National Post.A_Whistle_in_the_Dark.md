A flawless ensemble production of a relentlessly compelling play, written 44 years ago by the near-veteran Tom Murphy. Joseph Ziegler - - courtly, funny, piteous, scarifying -- plays an Irish patriarch who has raised his sons, now living in England, on a code of violence; he is great. Scarcely less so are Jonathan Goad as the one who tries to get away, Allan Hawco as tearaway-in-chief, Philip Riccio as kid-in-the-middle and, frankly, everybody. The Company Theatre has imported Irish director Jason Byrne, whose work is the tightest yet richest imaginable. Ends tonight. R.C.
