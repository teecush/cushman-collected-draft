Tarragon Extra Space - 30 Bridgman Ave. - 416-531-1827

Michael Redhill's play asks "why do good people do terrible things?" -- like commit genocide -- intelligently, absorbingly and frustratingly. The method is quasi-autobiograhical; Jordan Pettle plays a divorced Jewish writer (called Redhill) who stumbles on a tale about an ambiguous war-crimes trial in an unidentified country. But a play set everywhere is a play set nowhere. Closes tomorrow.
