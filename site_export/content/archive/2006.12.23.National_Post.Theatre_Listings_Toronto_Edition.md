ALADDIN THE MAGICAL FAMILY MUSICAL

Elgin Theatre

189 Yonge Street - 416-872-555

Kids who know Aladdin only through the Disney movie may be surprised to learn that the hero had a comic, widowed, cross-dressing mama and she had two funny, incompetent assistants. That may not be authentic Arabian Nights but it's pure English panto, as interpreted in Ross Pettys seasonal transplant, one of his best. Petty himself is, of course, the villain, and in the prologue a down- andout sheltering in a library; he acts this well. Jennifer Dale is the very sexy librarian. Let us pause to consider this universal fantasy. Now let us move on. Jamie McKnight is the charmingly gormless skateboarding hero, who rubs his lamp and gets a wrestler; thats Bret "The Hitman" Hart returning in the role of the genie. Robin Williams he isn't, but at least he's there in the flesh. Closes tomorrow.

A CHRISTMAS CAROL

Young Centre for

the Performing Arts 55 Mill St., Bldg. 49 - 416-866-8666

On view for the third time, Soulpeppers production, adapted and directed by Michael Shamata, fully captures Dickenss ability, not so much to make us laugh till we cry as to keep laughter and tears in play simultaneously. Joseph Ziegler's magnificent Scrooge is a fleshy, flinty businessman with, for better or worse, years of life still before him. His final joyous release is overwhelming. Oliver Dennis remains the prince of Cratchits. Closes tonight.

RADIO CITY CHRISTMAS SPECTACULAR

Hummingbird Centre

1 Front St. E. - 416-872-2262

The Rockettes have been performing their Parade of the Wooden Soldiers for decades, and it's some kind of high-precision classic. Everything else in this lavish Spectacular is utterly generic; its like nothing, and everything, youve ever seen. There's a sliver of continuity, about Santa Claus and how hardworked he is; something called Santa's Gonna Rock and Roll might be described, if one were feeling unseasonally uncharitable, as an abomination. The finale is an ultra-traditional involving three real camels (or are they dromedaries; as Ogden Nash wrote, "Im never sure, are you?"). Through Dec. 31.

WICKED

Canon Theatre

244 Victoria St. - 416-872-1212

A sort of parallel text to The Wizard of Oz but one in which narrative consistency goes by the board, music and lyrics are bombastic apart from a couple of nice tunes, and sets and costumes are cluttered. Its preachy but doesn't have the guts to follow through to the logical unhappy ending. Ding dong, the wicked old witch is dead -- but this one isn't wicked, old or even dead -- just misunderstood. Through Dec. 31.

SEUSSICAL -- A MUSICAL

Lorraine Kimsa Theatre

for Young People

165 Front St. E. - 416-862-2222

An economy version of Lynn Ahrenss and Stephen Flahertys Broadway musical based on the characters, stories and linguistic contortions of Dr. Seuss. It takes the audience's interest too much for granted (the Cat in the Hat seems to be there only because he couldn't be left out) but it can be charming, and it works once the basic narrative gets started. Lovely performances from George Masswohl and Corinne Koslo as Horton the elephant and Miss Gertrude McFuzz, respectively -- both, in their different ways, paragons of fidelity. Through Dec. 31.

DEAD AIR!

Mysteriously Yours

2026 Yonge St. - 416-486-7469

The plot and characters of the new Mysteriously Yours show are the same as the plot and characters of all their previous shows. But this time, as its subtitle says, is a musical murder mystery that sharpens the element of parody, here directed at old-time network radio presenting cowboy stars in things with titles like Withering Heights. (A Brontesaurus?) The songs are neat and all the cast have voices, including Jean Daigle doing his customary interactive turn as both corpse and sleuth. Indefinite run.

BIRD FLU OVER THE CUCKOO'S NEST

Second City

51 Mercer St. - 416-343-0011

The groan-title has a modicum of relevance; there is a number about avian flu, an East Indian calypso. Political satire fails, as usual, to materialize, but there are several goodies including Naomi Snieckus radiating every variety of shock and delight as a mother hacking into her son's MSN account. Indefinite run.
