Young Centre for the Performing Arts

55 Mill St. - 416-866-6666

The great virtue of Jim Warren's Soulpepper production is that nobody seems to have told it that Eugene Ionesco's minor classic is Absurd. Oliver Dennis and Kristen Thomson play the nonagenarian couple with a message for the world absolutely for real. They are also whizzes at the farcical mechanics of filling the bare stage with real chairs for imaginary auditors. The supposed demonstration of the meaninglessness of language is dated and unproved, but it's a superb portrait of a marriage. In rep through Sept. 6.

HARLEM DUET

Studio Theatre

Stratford - 1-800-567-1600

Djanet Sears' self-directed play about a modern New York Othello who leaves his black partner to marry his Desdemona. Being mainly domestic (with superfluous historical flashbacks), it doesn't sit too well on the thrust stage, but it's superbly written, with highly charged allusive dialogue and a compelling central relationship, excellently played by Nigel Shawn Williams and Karen Robinson. Other performances are lacking, and a layer of Canadian content seems like an add-on. In rep through Sept. 22.
