Jason Sherman's adaptation is probably as good a three-hour version of Dostoevsky's novel as anyone could come up with. It's a murder story, and the play is a continuous trial scene with a sporting, though probably foredoomed attempt to do justice to the ideas. But it can't shake the feeling that there's a book hanging in the wings. There are some weak performances, but for the most part Richard Rose's production is first-rate. Through Sept. 24.
