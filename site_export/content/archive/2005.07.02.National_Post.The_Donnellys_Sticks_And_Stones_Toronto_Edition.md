James Reaney's trilogy was probably very exciting if you were around at the time (1973). In this revival of its first instalment it seems so hung up on modes of storytelling that the story never gets told. Set around 1880, with many time shifts, you keep wanting the chorus actors to thin out so the others can interact. Robert King cannot make Mr. Donnelly more than a stocky malcontent; as his indomitable wife Diane D'Aquila is reduced to emoting into a void. Through Sept. 14.
