Berkeley Street Downstairs - 26 Berkeley St. * 416-368-3110

Chris Abraham's production has most of the musical and atmospheric trappings Tennessee Williams specified, but they come off more doomy than delicate. Rosemary Dunsmore's exaggerated Southern belle robs the play of its emotional impact. The best scene, of play and production, is between the emotionally crippled Laura and her gentleman caller, who may smash glass unicorns and fragile feelings, but is still the most likeable character: He means well. Until Feb. 16.
