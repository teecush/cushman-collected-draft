WICKED

Canon Theatre 244 Victoria St. - 416-872-1212

A sort of parallel text to The Wizard of Oz but one in which narrative consistency goes by the board, music and lyrics are bombastic apart from a couple of nice tunes, and sets and costumes are cluttered. It's preachy but doesnt have the guts to follow through to the logical unhappy ending. Ding dong, the wicked old witch is dead -- but this one isnt wicked, old or even dead -- just misunderstood. Through Dec. 31.

- - -

PIPPIN

Royal Alexandra Theatre 260 King St. W. - 416-872-1212

Stephen Schwartz's best score is affixed, like all the others, to a mawkish, pretentious show. Its hero (a charming Joshua Park) is the son of the Emperor Charlemagne in search of "a higher purpose." This revival suffers from de-Fossefication; Bob Fosse, the show's original director, pursuing his own higher purpose, gave it a brilliant staging that he then proceeded to tear down. Without his inventiveness and his obsessiveness, the piece is pointless. Through Dec. 3.

- - -

DESCENT

Theatre Passe Murialle 16 Ryerson Ave. - 416-504-7529

The descent is that of Susan whose desire for a pre-marital stag night, or its female equivalent, leads her eight years later to a nightmare night in an abandoned church in the comapny of four men, two of them named Randy and all of them lusting after her. Tom Walmsleys latest Skid Row excursion feels like a five-way monologue and takes for granted characters and relationships it hasnt established. Deborah Hay has style, especially in her jaded diatribes. Through Nov. 19.

- - -

HIGH SOCIETY

Festival Theatre Niagara-on-the-Lake 1-800-511-7429

The show of the movie musical of the play (that was also a movie). It never worked, and this busy production is particularly coarse. The augmented Cole Porter score is made up not of songs the characters just have to sing but of songs it's not quite impossible they might; it's dehydrating. Dan Chameroy and the sets are good. In rep through Nov. 19.

- - -

DESIGN FOR LIVING

Royal George Theatre Niagara-on-the-Lake 1-800-511-7429

Noel Coward wrote this menage-a-trois comedy as a vehicle for himself and his friends the Lunts, and it shows. Morris Panychs fine production, however, refuses to indulge the central threesome, losing a few laughs but gaining immensely in emotional reality. Nicole Underhays Gilda is a star performance without star tactics, a generous woman scared of whom she may be hurting; her partners, David Jansen and Graeme Somerville, are more mischievous, while Lorne Kennedy, the outsider, marvellously demonstrates the impotence of being Ernest. In rep through Nov. 18.

- - -

THE CARETAKER

Young Centre for the Performing Arts 55 Mill St., Bldg. 49 - 416- 866-8666

Soulpepper's best new production this year. (Our Town was a revival.) From the moment he comes on stage, twitching and blinking, Diego Matamoros's tramp, the cuckoo in Harold Pinters nest, is indelible. Damien Atkins is delicate and Matthew Edison ferally frightening as the brothers he tries to play against one another. Albert Schultzs direction of Pinter's 1960 masterpiece makes the power-shifts brilliantly clear. Through Nov. 25.

- - -

OF MICE AND MEN

Bluma Appel Theatre 27 Front St. E. - 416-368-3110

John Steinbeck's classic novel-into-play is devilishly well constructed, and Dennis Garnhums production is excellent, with magnificent sets, alternating stunning vistas of golden corn with timbered interiors. Shaun Smyth is an edgy George, Ashley Wright a trusting one-track Lennie, and theres a bunkhouseful of other good performances. The story shows the destruction of all best-laid plans, but play and production are proof that they can sometimes work out. Ends tonight.

- - -

DEAD AIR!

Mysteriously Yours 2026 Yonge St. - 416-486-7469

The plot and characters of the new Mysteriously Yours show are the same as the plot and characters of all their previous shows. But this time there's a new wrinkle; this, as its subtitle says, is a musical murder mystery that sharpens the element of parody, here directed at old-time network radio presenting cowboy stars in things with titles like Withering Heights. (A Brontesaurus?) The songs are neat and all the cast have voices, including Jean Daigle doing his customary interactive turn as both corpse and sleuth. Indefinite run.

- - -

BIRD FLU OVER THE CUCKOO'S NEST

Second City 51 Mercer St. - 416-343-0011

The groan-title has a modicum of relevance; there is a number about avian flu, an East Indian calypso. Political satire fails, as usual, to materialize, but there are several goodies including Naomi Snieckus radiating every variety of shock and delight as a mother hacking into her son's MSN account. Indefinite run.
