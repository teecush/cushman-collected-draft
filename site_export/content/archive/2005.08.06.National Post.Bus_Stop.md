Of its kind, as good as it gets. William Inge's 1955 Broadway hit calls up memories of a tidier theatrical time. As the naive cowboy and the floosie-chantoosie, Martin Happer and Nicole Underhay embody two American archetypes while bringing them to individual life. The equally archetypal roadside diner designed by Sue LePage looks good enough to be franchised. In repertory through Nov. 27.
