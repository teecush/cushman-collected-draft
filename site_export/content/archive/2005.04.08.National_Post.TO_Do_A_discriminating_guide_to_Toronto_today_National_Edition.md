THEATRE

AIN'T MISBEHAVIN'

At a time when musicals arrive with threatening titles like Wicked and Les Miserables, one welcomes the promised gaiety of Ain't Misbehavin', surely the cheekiest double negative in the annals of popular song. It was of course the title of one of Fats Waller's greatest hits as composer and performer (the other was Honeysuckle Rose) and it gives its name to one of the best-loved of all songwriter revues, not so much a biography as a celebration of his music and milieu. First staged in 1978, it's very much an ensemble piece; however, its revival by CanStage is a frank vehicle for Jackie Richardson following her success last season in Cookin' at the Cookery (and it has the same director, Marion J. Caffey). In Cookin', Richardson played Alberta Hunter; in Ain't Misbehavin' she plays Fats -- the whole cast does -- and it's a role to which, gender apart, she seems far better suited.
