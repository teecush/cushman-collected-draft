Court House Theatre

Niagara-on-the-Lake

Lillian Groag has written a presumably autobiographical play about a young woman who, like herself, grew up in Peron's Argentina and moved to the United States. Her seven-year-old self is watched by her narrating self, which splits the focus. Her immigrant family (Austrian and Italian) fill up the stage, mostly talking about opera, their defence against the world about them. The point is made many times. Lively compensating performances by Jennifer Phipps, Michael Ball, Ric Reid and others. In rep through Oct. 8.
