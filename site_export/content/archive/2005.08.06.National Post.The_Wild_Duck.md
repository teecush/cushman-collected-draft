One of the finest things Soulpepper, and director Laszlo Marton, have ever achieved. Ibsen's conflict between destructive ideals and saving illusions is the most moving play ever about cruelty to children, both young and grown-up. Great performances from Maggie MacIsaac as 14-year-old Hedvig, Brent Carver, Diego Matamoros -- actually the whole cast is impregnable. In repertory through Sept. 3.
